Spiral Edit - video editor

This is the beginning code sketch for a video
editor where the timeline is arranged in the form
of a rectangular spiral instead of a horizontal track. 
The playhead moves around the spiral starting
from the center, which is the beginning of the timeline.

Because of the expanded view provided for browsing 
the video sequence, two timelines are provided: 
frames and clips.  Frames is all of the frames of all of
the videos.  Clips is the clips as they are
shown now in a video editor timeline

A rectangular spiral UI provides a lot of
benefits for navigating and editing videos,
first because it removes fatigue of having to
seek to the desired location on a timeline.
On a horizontal timeline, only a portion of 
the entire sequence of videos can be
shown because the UI. Using a rectangular spiral
timeline instead involves zooming
out and zooming in like in CAD.  

Another benefit is that there is value in
having another preview box underneath the
playhead preview box which is one that updates
based on where the mouse hovers. 

A timeline is natural
for physical film editing but when the
computer display is available film editing
should be adapted to its conventions, namely
the ability (especially in the last decade)
to zoom in and out. This also demonstrates
the importance of changing the form of a
technology when the surrounding conditions
have changed.
